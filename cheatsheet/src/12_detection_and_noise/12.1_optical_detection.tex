\subsection*{Optical Detection Processes}
\subsubsection*{Quantum Detectors}

Quantum detectors respond to the number of absorbed photons.

\begin{minipage}[t]{0.48\linewidth}
\textbf{Photon rate:}
\[
N_{\text{ph}} = \frac{\eta P}{h\nu}
= \frac{\eta P \lambda}{hc}
\]

\textbf{Cut-off wavelength:}
\[
\lambda_{\max}(\mu\mathrm{m})
= \frac{1.24}{E(\mathrm{eV})}
\]
\end{minipage}
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.4\linewidth}
\vspace{0pt}
\tiny
$N_{\text{ph}}$ : detected photon rate $\left[\mathrm{s^{-1}}\right]$ \\
$P$ : optical power $\left[\mathrm{W}\right]$ \\
$\eta$ : quantum efficiency $[-]$ \\
$h$ : Planck constant $\left[\mathrm{J\,s}\right]$ \\
$\nu$ : optical frequency $\left[\mathrm{Hz}\right]$ \\
$\lambda$ : wavelength $\left[\mathrm{m}\right]$ \\
$c$ : speed of light $\left[\mathrm{m/s}\right]$ \\
$E$ : semiconductor bandgap $\left[\mathrm{eV}\right]$ \\
\end{minipage}

\textbf{Wavelength dependence:}
\[
\lambda \uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
N_{\text{ph}}/P \uparrow
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{higher electrical response}
\]

\subsubsection*{Thermal Detectors}

Thermal detectors respond to absorbed energy rather than photon number.
Their spectral response is set by the absorber coating.
Response time is slow due to heat conversion.
